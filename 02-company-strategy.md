# AI-Native Product Company Strategy

## 1. Identity

We are an AI-native product company building real-world, revenue-generating products — while developing and deploying a disciplined AI Studio that enables repeated product creation, automation, and scale.

The AI Studio is the fuel.
Products are the destination.

---

## 2. Core Thesis

AI fundamentally changes the economics of product creation.

Small, disciplined teams can now:

- Design faster
- Automate intelligently
- Launch repeatedly
- Scale with fewer resources

We are building:

1. Market-facing AI-powered products.
2. The internal AI Studio that makes repeatable product creation possible.

---

## 3. Strategic Structure

The company operates under one unified umbrella with two engines:

### 3.1 Product Engine (Destination)

Focus Areas:

- Trust-centered AI systems
- Aging and care visibility platforms (e.g., VisitMe)
- Access and identity systems (e.g., AI Doorman)
- Human-in-the-loop AI infrastructure in physical environments

Goals:

- Build IP
- File provisional patents
- Secure grants
- Pilot early deployments
- Establish SaaS or recurring revenue

---

### 3.2 AI Studio (Fuel Engine)

Purpose:

- Accelerate internal product development
- Deploy AI automation into operational environments
- Generate capital to fund R&D
- Harden and refine AI governance architecture

The Studio:

- Is deployable
- Is portable (local or cloud)
- Is designed for repeatability
- May become a standalone product in the future

---

## 4. Revenue Model (Phase 1 – 12 Months)

Primary Revenue:

- AI Studio deployments in business environments
- Structured AI workflow automation programs
- High-leverage implementation engagements

Secondary Objectives:

- Strengthen Studio architecture
- Generate case studies
- Identify repeatable patterns

Product revenue is expected in Phase 2+.

---

## 5. Product Development Phasing

### Phase 1 (0–12 months)

- Monetize Studio deployments
- Build VisitMe MVP (software-first)
- File provisional patents
- Submit at least one grant

### Phase 2 (12–24 months)

- Pilot VisitMe in controlled environments
- Integrate selective edge AI modules
- Seek strategic funding
- Expand team cautiously

### Phase 3 (24–36 months)

- Expand product modules
- Evaluate hardware extensions (e.g., medication device)
- Consider Studio productization if market validates

---

## 6. Studio Positioning

We do not sell generic consulting.

We deploy our AI Studio inside operational environments.

The Studio:

- Implements controlled AI systems
- Runs locally or in cloud
- Maintains human-in-the-loop governance
- Uses tiered intelligence (local-first, cloud escalation)

---

## 7. Strategic Guardrails

- Studio must strengthen product capability.
- Revenue must not eliminate product time.
- Product IP takes precedence over service customization.
- Architecture must remain modular and portable.
- Avoid hardware complexity until product validation.

---

## 8. Long-Term Optionality

Possible Futures:

1. Edge product (VisitMe) becomes primary revenue engine.
2. AI Studio becomes scalable platform offering.
3. Hybrid model with both growing in parallel.

The strategy preserves all three options.
